Understand what platforms collect & how to protect yourself

We break down policies into clear summaries, show a quick risk rating, and give practical steps you can take if something goes wrong.

TikTok — What this platform collects

Policy applies to users 13+. Summary of the main data categories TikTok collects.

  • Account info (name, age, email)
  • User-generated content (videos, comments, messages)
  • Payment details, contests/surveys, ID verification documents
  • Linked third-party accounts (Google, Facebook)
  • Advertisers, affiliates, public records, mentions
  • Device details, IP address, cookies, metadata
  • Approximate location (U.S.), clipboard (with permission)
  • Biometric identifiers (face/voice) with consent; engagement patterns

If your account is compromised

1
Secure Account
2
Review Connections
3
Report & Document
  1. Change your password & enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  2. Revoke connected apps and unlink social accounts; document impersonation evidence.
  3. Report to TikTok support and follow recovery steps.

Risk Level Key
High — Major collection & sharing
Collects identity, messages, contacts, biometrics, device & browsing data; shares with advertisers, affiliates & partners; used for ad personalization and AI training.
Medium — Moderate collection
Collects many data types but shares less externally and may limit retention or cross-platform tracking.
Low — Minimal collection
Only essential account data; limited tracking and sharing.

Instagram — What this platform collects

Policy applies to users 13+. Summary of main categories Instagram/Meta collects and shares.

  • Account info: username, profile photo, phone number, bio
  • User-generated content: photos, videos, stories, DMs
  • Contacts you upload via "Find Friends"
  • Device identifiers, IP, cookies, pixels, log file info
  • Location (exact GPS if allowed), analytics & usage patterns
  • Camera & photo access when granted
  • Data from Meta affiliates, advertisers, and third-party apps

If something goes wrong

1
Secure Account
2
Review Activity
3
Report & Document
  1. Change password, enable 2FA, and check active sessions.
  2. Remove connected apps and review account activity for unauthorized access.
  3. Report impersonation and keep screenshots as evidence.

Risk Level Key
High — Major collection & sharing
Collects identity, device & analytics data and shares across Meta services and advertisers; used for cross-app tracking and profiling.
Medium — Moderate collection
Collects many types but sharing is mostly within the Meta ecosystem or to partners.
Low — Minimal collection
Only essential data and limited sharing with external parties.

Snapchat — What this platform collects

Snapchat collects a range of user and device data; below are common categories and quick actions.

  • Account info (username, phone, email)
  • User-generated content: Snaps, Stories, Messages, Memories
  • Contacts you choose to upload
  • Linked apps (Bitmoji, Snap Kit), ad/analytics partners
  • Device details, IP, cookies, sensor data
  • Approximate or precise location (GPS/Wi-Fi)
  • Engagement patterns and metadata (filters used)

If you suspect an account breach

1
Secure Account
2
Download Data
3
Report & Document
  1. Change password, enable 2FA, and log out of other devices.
  2. Download your data if needed and document impersonation evidence.
  3. Report to Snapchat support and follow recovery steps.

Risk Level Key
High — Major collection & sharing
Collects identity & device data and shares with advertisers & analytics partners; used for ad targeting and measurement.
Medium — Moderate collection
Collects location and engagement data; shares with partners but often retains shorter retention and fewer cross-app integrations.
Low — Minimal collection
Only essential data and limited sharing.
