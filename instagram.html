Instagram

Breakdown of data practices and straightforward steps to protect your presence.

📦 What Instagram collects

Provided by you

  • Account info: username, profile photo, bio
  • User content: posts, stories, DMs
  • Contacts you choose to upload

Automatically collected

  • Device identifiers, IP, cookies, pixels
  • Location (exact GPS if allowed)
  • Usage & analytics (engagement patterns)

From other sources

  • Data from Meta affiliates
  • Advertisers & third-party partners

⚙️ How Instagram uses data

  • Personalized content & advertisements
  • Product development, analytics, and tests
  • To verify accounts & enforce policies

🔁 How data is shared

Instagram shares data within Meta and with service providers, advertisers, and in response to legal requests.

🚦 Risk Rating

Instagram collects a broad set of data and shares across Meta services — pay attention to connected apps and profile visibility.

🛡️ How to protect your Instagram

  1. Use the Account Privacy toggle to switch to a private account (Profiles → Settings → Privacy).
  2. Review “Apps and Websites” to remove third-party integrations.
  3. Turn off precise location and limit camera/photo permissions.
  4. Use strong passwords and enable 2FA.
