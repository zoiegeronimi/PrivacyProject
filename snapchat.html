Snapchat — What data is collected

  • Account info (username, phone, email)
  • User-generated content: Snaps, Stories, messages
  • Contacts you choose to upload
  • Linked apps (Bitmoji, Snap Kit), mentions by users, ad partners
  • Device details, IP, cookies, analytics
  • Approximate or precise location (GPS/Wi-Fi)
  • Engagement patterns and metadata (which filters you use)
Risk Level Key
High — Major collection & sharing
Collects extensive personal & device data and shares widely with advertisers, analytics partners, and for business purposes.
Medium — Moderate collection
Collects location and engagement data; shares with partners but often retains shorter retention and fewer cross-platform integrations compared to the highest-risk platforms.
Low — Minimal collection
Only essential data and limited sharing.

Quick actions

  1. Enable Ghost Mode in Snap Map to hide real-time location.
  2. Set Stories to Friends or Custom and disable Quick Add.
  3. Turn off contact syncing if you want fewer matches.
  4. Enable login verification (2FA).

If you suspect an account breach

  1. Change password, enable 2FA, and log out open sessions.
  2. Download your data if needed and document evidence of impersonation.
  3. Report to Snapchat support and follow their recommended recovery steps.
